# First Night Run — Longest Recorded Run

## Summary

Today started as a simple experiment to see how running at night would feel.

Unexpectedly, it became one of the most memorable runs of the training journey so far.

The weather felt comfortable, the effort felt controlled, and running at night turned out to be much more enjoyable than expected. Breathing remained comfortable throughout the run and the pace felt sustainable.

Most importantly, this run became the longest officially recorded run in the journal.

## Special Notes

During the workout, the tracking app was accidentally paused without being noticed.

The run should have ended approximately 20–21 minutes earlier than the recorded finish time, but the workout continued while the app was paused.

Because of this, the official recorded distance remains **7.60 km**.

However, based on the average pace and estimated paused duration, the actual distance covered may have been significantly longer and potentially close to **10 km**.

This estimate is intentionally not counted as an official milestone because the GPS data was not fully recorded.
