# 1K Repeats Session

## Summary

Today was my first structured interval workout from the Runna Half Marathon training plan.

Before starting the run, motivation was quite low and I felt reluctant to begin the session. However, after completing the first kilometer, my mood improved significantly and the workout started to feel enjoyable.

Although a few intervals were slightly faster than the prescribed pace, the entire session was completed successfully and felt controlled throughout.

## Planned Workout

### Warm Up

- 1.5 km easy run
- 90 seconds recovery

### Main Set

- 3 × 1 km repeats
- Target pace: 8:15/km
- 90 seconds recovery after each repeat

### Cool Down

- 1.5 km easy run

Target total distance: approximately 6 km

## Actual Result

- Distance: 6.49 km
- Duration: 56:39
- Average Pace: 8:44/km
- Average HR: 149 bpm
- Max HR: 169 bpm
- Cadence: 160 spm

## Reflection

The hardest part of today's workout was not the intervals themselves but getting started.

Before the run, I felt unmotivated and mentally resistant to training. However, after the first kilometer, my body settled into a comfortable rhythm and the session became much more enjoyable.

The interval portions felt challenging but manageable, and I was able to maintain effort throughout the workout.

This was also my first experience following a more structured running workout compared to previous easy runs and long runs.

## Key Takeaways

- Successfully completed the entire interval session.
- Mental resistance before starting was harder than the workout itself.
- Body is adapting to pace variations and structured training.
- Intervals felt challenging but enjoyable.
- Recovery periods were sufficient to maintain workout quality.
